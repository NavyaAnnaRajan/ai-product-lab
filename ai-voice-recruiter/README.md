## 💡 Why I built this

Recruiters spend a considerable amount of time conducting repetitive screening interviews—asking the same qualification questions, documenting responses, and deciding whether candidates should progress to the next stage. As hiring volumes increase, this process becomes difficult to scale while still providing a consistent experience for every candidate.

With recent advancements in Voice AI and Large Language Models, I wanted to explore whether the initial screening process could be redesigned as an AI-first experience. Rather than building another chatbot, my goal was to understand how AI could conduct structured conversations, capture meaningful candidate insights, and support recruiters with transparent recommendations instead of replacing their decision-making.

This project is my exploration of what an enterprise-ready AI recruitment assistant could look like—from product strategy and user experience to AI architecture, governance, and evaluation.

---

## 🚀 What the assistant does

The assistant conducts structured voice interviews with candidates, understands responses in real time, evaluates answers against job-specific requirements, and generates concise summaries for recruiters.

Instead of simply transcribing conversations, the system is designed to:

- 🎙️ Conduct natural voice-based screening interviews.
- 🧠 Understand candidate responses using Large Language Models.
- 📄 Evaluate candidate fit against job requirements.
- 💬 Ask contextual follow-up questions during the conversation.
- 📊 Generate structured recruiter summaries instead of raw transcripts.
- 🔍 Provide explainable reasoning behind recommendations.
- 👤 Keep recruiters in control through human review and approval.

## 🌱 Key Takeaways

This project reinforced my belief that the value of AI products lies not only in model capabilities but in thoughtful product design. Building trustworthy AI experiences requires balancing automation with human oversight, designing for transparency, evaluating model quality continuously, and creating workflows that integrate naturally into existing business processes. Exploring these trade-offs helped me better understand how Product Managers can design AI-native products that are practical, reliable, and ready for enterprise adoption.
